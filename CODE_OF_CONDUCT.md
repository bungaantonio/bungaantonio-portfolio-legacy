# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

This project values respectful, serious, and constructive collaboration.

The expectation is simple: interact in a way that supports clear thinking, responsible work, and mutual respect.

## Expected Behavior

- communicate with respect, even in disagreement
- critique ideas and implementations without diminishing people
- be precise, honest, and constructive
- make space for learning, correction, and improvement
- prioritize clarity over hostility or performance

## Unacceptable Behavior

- harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks
- dismissive or degrading language
- bad-faith communication
- deliberate disruption of discussion or collaboration
- any behavior that makes participation unsafe or unnecessarily hostile

## Collaboration Principle

Good engineering work requires more than technical ability. It also requires discipline in how people communicate, review, and disagree.

This means:

- assumptions can be questioned
- decisions can be challenged
- mistakes can be corrected

But all of that should happen without disrespect.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to repository discussions, issues, pull requests, reviews, and other project-related interactions.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reviewed and addressed by the project maintainer.

Responses may include:

- requesting a change in behavior
- limiting participation in discussion
- rejecting contributions
- removing access where necessary

The goal of enforcement is not performance or image. It is to preserve a working environment where people can contribute seriously and safely.

## Final Note

I care about technical work that is reliable in practice. The same standard applies to collaboration: it should be steady, respectful, and able to hold under pressure.
